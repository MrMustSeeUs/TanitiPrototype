ABOUT TANITI ISLAND

ISLAND OVERVIEW

  • Small tropical island (less than 500 square miles)
  • Varied terrain: beaches, harbor, rainforest, mountains, and active volcano
  • Indigenous population of about 20,000
  • Traditional economy: fishing and agriculture
  • Growing tourism industry

GEOGRAPHY & NATURE

BEACHES

Taniti features both sandy and rocky beaches surrounding the island, with the most popular white sand beaches encircling Yellow Leaf Bay.

RAINFOREST

Our lush tropical rainforests cover much of the island's interior, offering hiking trails and zip-lining adventures.

VOLCANO

The small but active volcano in our mountainous interior is a popular destination for guided tours and photography.

CULTURE & HISTORY

Indigenous Traditions

The people of Taniti have maintained their rich cultural heritage for centuries, with traditions centered around fishing, agriculture, and community celebrations.

Local Customs

Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, including modest dress when away from beaches and participation in traditional greetings.

Historical Timeline

  • Pre-1700s: Indigenous settlement and development
  • 1800s: First European contact and limited trade
  • Early 1900s: Development of fishing industry
  • 1950s: Agricultural expansion
  • 1990s: Beginning of tourism industry
  • Present: Balanced development with cultural preservation

Cultural Attractions

Visit our local history museum, art galleries, and cultural centers to experience authentic Tanitian traditions and crafts.

TANITI CITY

  • Main urban center of the island
  • Featuring beautiful native architecture
  • Located on the shores of Yellow Leaf Bay
  • Primary shopping and dining hub
  • Walkable city center with public transportation
  • Home to many cultural attractions and events

MERRITON LANDING

  • Rapidly developing area on the north side of Yellow Leaf Bay
  • Entertainment center with pubs, dance club, and microbrewery
  • Home to many water activities and tour operators
  • New 9-hole golf course opening next year
  • Popular area for tourists with many accommodation options
  • Easy to explore on foot
