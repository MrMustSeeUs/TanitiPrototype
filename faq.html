Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about visiting Taniti Island

Travel Planning

Do I need a visa to visit Taniti Island?

Visitors from most countries do not need a visa for stays under 90 days. However, you will need a valid passport with at least six months validity beyond your planned departure date. Please check with your country's travel authority or the Taniti Tourism Board for specific requirements based on your nationality.

What is the best time of year to visit Taniti?

Taniti enjoys a tropical climate year-round with temperatures ranging from 75°F to 90°F (24°C to 32°C). The dry season from April to October is generally considered the best time to visit, with less rainfall and humidity. The peak tourist season is from June to August.

The rainy season from November to March brings occasional heavy showers, but they typically pass quickly. This period offers fewer crowds and lower prices, making it attractive for budget travelers.

What currency is used on Taniti Island?

The official currency is the Taniti Dollar (TND), but US dollars are widely accepted throughout the island. Major credit cards are accepted at hotels, larger restaurants, and tour operators, but it's advisable to carry cash for smaller establishments and markets.

ATMs are available in the main town and at the airport. We recommend exchanging some currency upon arrival for convenience.

What languages are spoken on Taniti?

The official language is Tanitian, but English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. Many locals also speak some Spanish and French. Learning a few basic Tanitian phrases is appreciated by locals and can enhance your cultural experience.

Common phrases include:

  • Hello - Halo
  • Thank you - Mahalo
  • Please - Paliki
  • Yes/No - Ae/Aole

What is the weather like on Taniti?

Taniti has a tropical climate with warm temperatures year-round. The average daily temperature ranges from 75°F to 90°F (24°C to 32°C). The island has two main seasons:

  • Dry Season (April-October): Less rainfall, lower humidity, and consistent sunshine.
  • Rainy Season (November-March): Brief but intense afternoon showers, higher humidity, and occasional tropical storms.

The ocean temperature averages 80°F (27°C) year-round, making it perfect for swimming and water activities in any season.

What should I pack for my trip to Taniti?

For a comfortable visit to Taniti, we recommend packing:

  • Lightweight, breathable clothing (cotton, linen)
  • Swimwear and beach cover-ups
  • Sun protection (hat, sunglasses, reef-safe sunscreen)
  • Insect repellent
  • Light rain jacket or umbrella (especially during rainy season)
  • Comfortable walking shoes and sandals
  • One light sweater or jacket for cooler evenings
  • Basic first-aid supplies
  • Reusable water bottle

If you plan to hike or visit the volcano, bring appropriate footwear and a light jacket as temperatures can be cooler at higher elevations.

Are there any health concerns or vaccinations needed?

No specific vaccinations are required to enter Taniti, but we recommend being up-to-date on routine vaccinations. The CDC and WHO recommend hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations for most travelers to tropical destinations.

Taniti has good quality drinking water in most tourist areas, but bottled water is widely available if preferred. The island has a modern hospital in the main town and several clinics throughout the island.

We recommend purchasing travel insurance that includes medical coverage before your trip.

Accommodations

What types of accommodations are available on Taniti?

Taniti offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference:

  • Luxury Resorts: Beachfront properties with full amenities, spas, and multiple dining options.
  • Boutique Hotels: Smaller, unique properties with personalized service.
  • Vacation Rentals: Private homes and villas, ideal for families or longer stays.
  • Eco-Lodges: Sustainable accommodations integrated with nature, often in more remote areas.
  • Budget Hotels & Hostels: Affordable options with basic amenities.
  • Bed & Breakfasts: Family-run establishments offering a more authentic local experience.

Most accommodations are concentrated in the main town and along the eastern and northern beaches.

What are the best areas to stay on the island?

The best area to stay depends on your interests and preferences:

  • Main Town: Convenient access to shopping, dining, and cultural attractions. Best for those who want to be close to amenities and nightlife.
  • Eastern Beaches: Home to most luxury resorts and family-friendly beaches with calm waters. Ideal for relaxation and water activities.
  • Northern Coast: More secluded beaches and boutique accommodations. Perfect for couples seeking romance and privacy.
  • Western Region: Rural area with eco-lodges and access to hiking trails. Great for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
  • Southern Peninsula: Emerging area with newer resorts and less crowded beaches. Good balance of amenities and tranquility.

How far in advance should I book my accommodation?

We recommend booking your accommodation:

  • Peak Season (June-August): 4-6 months in advance, especially for luxury resorts and popular properties.
  • Shoulder Season (April-May, September-October): 2-3 months in advance.
  • Low Season (November-March): 1-2 months in advance is usually sufficient, except for holiday periods.

For special occasions or if you have specific requirements, booking further in advance is advisable. Last-minute bookings are possible during low season but may limit your options.

Are there family-friendly accommodations?

Taniti offers many family-friendly accommodation options. Several resorts and hotels provide:

  • Family suites and connecting rooms
  • Kids' clubs and supervised activities
  • Child-friendly pools and beach areas
  • Children's menus at restaurants
  • Babysitting services

Vacation rentals are also excellent for families, offering multiple bedrooms, kitchen facilities, and more space. The eastern beach area is particularly popular with families due to its calm waters and proximity to family-friendly activities.

Are there eco-friendly or sustainable accommodation options?

Yes, Taniti has several eco-friendly accommodation options that emphasize sustainability and minimal environmental impact. These include:

  • Eco-Lodges: Built with sustainable materials and designed to blend with the natural environment.
  • Green-Certified Hotels: Properties that have received certification for their environmental practices.
  • Solar-Powered Bungalows: Off-grid accommodations that use renewable energy.

Many eco-friendly properties also feature organic gardens, water conservation systems, farm-to-table dining, and educational programs about local ecology. These accommodations are primarily located in the western and southern regions of the island.

Activities

What are the must-do activities on Taniti?

Taniti offers a wide range of unforgettable experiences. Here are the top activities we recommend:

  • Snorkeling or diving at Coral Reef Marine Park: Explore vibrant coral gardens and encounter tropical fish, sea turtles, and rays.
  • Hiking to Taniti Volcano: Take a guided tour to the dormant volcano for breathtaking panoramic views of the island.
  • Visiting the Cultural Center: Learn about Taniti's rich heritage through interactive exhibits and traditional performances.
  • Exploring the Rainforest: Join a guided tour to discover exotic plants, birds, and wildlife in the island's lush interior.
  • Relaxing on Yellow Leaf Beach: Enjoy the island's most famous beach with its golden sands and crystal-clear waters.
  • Taking a Sunset Cruise: Experience the magical Taniti sunset from the water, often with dinner and entertainment included.
  • Shopping at the Local Market: Browse handcrafted souvenirs, tropical fruits, and local specialties at the vibrant market in the main town.

What water activities are available?

Taniti's crystal-clear waters offer excellent conditions for a variety of water activities:

  • Snorkeling: Accessible from many beaches, with equipment rental widely available.
  • Scuba Diving: Several PADI-certified dive centers offer courses and guided dives for all experience levels.
  • Surfing: The western beaches have good surf breaks, with lessons and board rentals available.
  • Paddleboarding & Kayaking: Popular in the calmer eastern waters, with guided tours available.
  • Sailing: Charter boats or join group sailing excursions around the island.
  • Fishing: Deep-sea fishing charters target marlin, tuna, and mahi-mahi.
  • Jet Skiing & Parasailing: Available at the main beaches in the eastern region.
  • Glass-Bottom Boat Tours: Perfect for those who want to see marine life without getting wet.

Water activity operators typically provide all necessary equipment and safety instructions.

What activities are suitable for families with children?

Taniti offers many family-friendly activities that children of all ages will enjoy:

  • Taniti Aquarium: Interactive exhibits featuring local marine life with touch pools and feeding demonstrations.
  • Wildlife Sanctuary: See native animals up close with guided tours designed for children.
  • Banana Boat Rides: Exciting water activity suitable for children accompanied by adults.
  • Cultural Craft Workshops: Learn traditional crafts like weaving and pottery making.
  • Gentle Hiking Trails: Several short, easy trails suitable for families with viewpoints and picnic areas.
  • Botanical Gardens: Explore tropical plants with kid-friendly scavenger hunts and activities.
  • Beach Activities: Building sand castles, collecting shells, and swimming in the calm eastern beaches.
  • Mini-Golf: Tropical-themed course located near the main town.

Many resorts also offer kids' clubs with supervised activities, allowing parents some relaxation time.

What cultural experiences are available?

Immerse yourself in Taniti's rich cultural heritage through these experiences:

  • Taniti Cultural Center: Museum exhibits, traditional dance performances, and historical displays.
  • Village Tours: Visit authentic villages to learn about daily life and traditions from local guides.
  • Traditional Cooking Classes: Learn to prepare Tanitian dishes using local ingredients and techniques.
  • Craft Workshops: Try your hand at traditional crafts like weaving, carving, or pottery.
  • Cultural Festivals: If your visit coincides with local festivals, you'll experience music, dance, and celebrations.
  • Historical Sites: Guided tours of ancient temples and colonial-era buildings.
  • Traditional Fishing Demonstration: Learn about traditional fishing methods still practiced today.

Many cultural experiences include opportunities to interact with local artisans and community members, providing authentic insights into Tanitian culture.

Are there hiking trails on the island?

Taniti offers a variety of hiking trails for all skill levels, showcasing the island's diverse landscapes:

  • Volcano Trail: Moderate 4-hour hike to the summit of Taniti's dormant volcano with panoramic views (guided tours recommended).
  • Rainforest Loop: Easy 2-hour walk through lush jungle with opportunities to spot wildlife and exotic plants.
  • Coastal Path: Easy to moderate 6-mile trail following the northern coastline with beach access points and viewpoints.
  • Waterfall Trek: Moderate 3-hour hike to a series of cascading waterfalls with swimming opportunities.
  • Ridge Trail: Challenging full-day hike along the island's central ridge with spectacular views (experienced hikers only).
  • Botanical Path: Easy 1-hour walk through gardens showcasing native plant species.

For safety, we recommend hiking with a guide for more challenging trails, wearing appropriate footwear, carrying water, and informing someone of your plans. Trail maps are available at the Visitor Center and most hotels.

Do I need to book activities in advance?

For many activities on Taniti, advance booking is recommended, especially during the peak season (June-August). Here's a general guide:

  • Popular Tours (volcano hikes, cultural tours, sunset cruises): Book 1-2 weeks in advance during peak season, a few days ahead in low season.
  • Diving Excursions: Book 3-5 days in advance, especially for specific dive sites or certification courses.
  • Spa Treatments: Book 2-3 days ahead at resort spas, especially for popular time slots.
  • Fishing Charters: Book 1 week in advance as boats are limited.
  • Restaurant Reservations (for fine dining): Book 2-3 days ahead for popular restaurants.

More casual activities like beach equipment rental, basic snorkeling, and self-guided hiking typically don't require advance booking. Your hotel concierge can assist with most activity bookings, often at no extra charge.

Transportation

How do I get to Taniti Island?

Taniti is accessible by air and sea:

  • By Air: Taniti International Airport (TAN) receives direct flights from major cities in the United States, Australia, New Zealand, and several Asian countries. Connection flights are available from other international destinations.
  • By Sea: Cruise ships visit Taniti as part of their Pacific itineraries, typically docking for 1-2 days. Private boats can also dock at the marina in the main town, subject to customs clearance.

The airport is located approximately 10 miles (16 km) from the main town, with taxi and shuttle services readily available for transfers to your accommodation.

What's the best way to get around the island?

Taniti offers several transportation options for exploring the island:

  • Rental Cars: The most flexible option for exploring at your own pace. International driver's licenses are accepted. Driving is on the left side of the road.
  • Scooters & Motorcycles: Popular for individuals or couples. Helmet use is mandatory.
  • Bicycles: Great for short distances and exploring the flatter coastal areas. Many hotels offer free or low-cost rentals.
  • Public Buses: Affordable option connecting major towns and beaches. Schedules can be limited, especially in the evening.
  • Taxis: Readily available in the main town and at major hotels. Agree on the fare before departure or ensure the meter is used.
  • Ride-sharing: Mobile apps for ride-sharing are available in the main town and popular tourist areas.
  • Water Taxis: Connect coastal areas and nearby small islands.

For visitors staying at resorts, many offer shuttle services to popular destinations or can arrange transportation for you.

Can I drive on Taniti? What are the requirements?

Yes, visitors can drive on Taniti with the following requirements:

  • Valid driver's license from your home country
  • International Driving Permit (IDP) recommended but not mandatory for most nationalities
  • Minimum age of 21 to rent a vehicle (25 for some rental companies)
  • Credit card for the security deposit

Important driving information:

  • Driving is on the left side of the road
  • Speed limits: 30 mph (50 km/h) in towns, 50 mph (80 km/h) on highways
  • Seatbelts are mandatory for all passengers
  • Helmets are required for motorcycle and scooter riders
  • Mobile phone use while driving is prohibited unless using hands-free

Road conditions vary, with well-maintained roads in tourist areas and more challenging conditions in remote parts of the island. During the rainy season, some rural roads may become difficult to navigate.

Is there public transportation on the island?

Yes, Taniti has a public bus system that serves the main town and popular tourist areas:

  • Main Routes: Connect the airport, main town, major beaches, and popular attractions
  • Schedule: Buses run approximately every 30 minutes from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM on main routes
  • Fares: Affordable flat-rate fares (approximately $2 USD per trip) with day passes available ($8 USD)
  • Payment: Cash only (local currency or US dollars) paid directly to the driver

The buses are recognizable by their bright blue color and "Taniti Transit" logo. Bus stops are marked with blue signs throughout the island. A route map and schedule are available at the airport, visitor center, and most hotels.

While convenient for major destinations, public transportation may not reach some remote areas or provide the flexibility needed for extensive exploration.

How do I get from the airport to my accommodation?

Several options are available for transfers from Taniti International Airport to your accommodation:

  • Hotel Shuttles: Many larger hotels and resorts offer complimentary or paid shuttle services. Check with your accommodation before arrival and book in advance.
  • Taxis: Available directly outside the terminal. The fare to the main town is approximately $20-25 USD, with higher rates for more distant locations. Credit cards are accepted by most taxi drivers.
  • Shared Shuttles: More economical option with multiple stops. Tickets can be purchased at the transportation desk in the arrival hall for around $10-15 USD per person to the main town.
  • Public Bus: The most affordable option at $2 USD. The bus stop is a 5-minute walk from the terminal, with service to the main town every 30 minutes.
  • Car Rentals: Several agencies have counters at the airport if you prefer to drive yourself.

The journey from the airport to the main town takes approximately 20-30 minutes, depending on traffic. For accommodations in other areas, travel time can range from 15 minutes to over an hour.

How large is the island and how long does it take to travel around it?

Taniti is approximately 50 square miles (130 square kilometers) in size. The island is roughly oval-shaped, measuring about 15 miles (24 km) at its longest point and 8 miles (13 km) at its widest.

Travel times around the island:

  • Driving: A complete circuit of the coastal road takes approximately 3-4 hours with no stops. From the main town to the furthest point on the island is about 45 minutes.
  • Public Bus: A full circuit on the coastal route takes about 5 hours due to frequent stops.
  • Bicycle: Cycling the coastal road would take a full day (8-10 hours) for most recreational cyclists.

While Taniti is relatively small, the varied terrain, winding roads, and numerous attractions mean that most visitors spend at least 5-7 days exploring the island to avoid feeling rushed.

Dining

What is traditional Tanitian cuisine like?

Traditional Tanitian cuisine is a flavorful blend of Pacific Island, Asian, and European influences, featuring fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and locally grown vegetables. Key elements include:

  • Seafood: The foundation of many dishes, including fish marinated in citrus juices (similar to ceviche), grilled octopus, and coconut milk-based fish stews.
  • Starches: Taro root, sweet potatoes, and breadfruit prepared in various ways, from simple boiling to complex fermentations.
  • Tropical Fruits: Papaya, mango, pineapple, and lesser-known local fruits feature in both savory dishes and desserts.
  • Coconut: Used in nearly every form—the milk, cream, flesh, and oil are all common ingredients.
  • Spices: Ginger, chili, lemongrass, and vanilla are prominent flavors.

Signature dishes to try include:

  • Kokoda: Fresh fish marinated in lime juice and coconut cream with vegetables
  • Palusami: Taro leaves wrapped around coconut cream and onions, baked in an earth oven
  • Taniti Curry: A unique seafood curry with local spices and coconut milk
  • Lovo: Meats and vegetables slow-cooked in an underground earth oven

What types of restaurants are available?

Taniti offers a diverse dining scene to suit all tastes and budgets:

  • Fine Dining: Several upscale restaurants, primarily in luxury resorts, offering gourmet cuisine with local and international influences. Reservations recommended.
  • Seafood Restaurants: Specializing in fresh, locally caught fish and shellfish, often with beachfront settings.
  • Traditional Tanitian: Family-run establishments serving authentic local cuisine, often with cultural performances in the evening.
  • International Cuisine: Restaurants offering Asian, European, and American dishes, concentrated in the main town and tourist areas.
  • Cafés & Bakeries: Serving breakfast, light meals, coffee, and pastries, many featuring tropical fruits and local ingredients.
  • Beach Bars & Grills: Casual dining with sandwiches, salads, and grilled items, perfect for lunch during a beach day.
  • Food Trucks & Street Food: Offering affordable local specialties, found in the main town and at popular beaches.

Most restaurants are concentrated in the main town and along the eastern and northern coasts. In more remote areas, dining options may be limited to resort restaurants and local eateries with simpler menus.

Can the island accommodate dietary restrictions?

Yes, most restaurants on Taniti can accommodate common dietary restrictions, though the level of understanding and options vary by establishment:

  • Vegetarian: Well-understood and catered for at most restaurants, with many traditional dishes naturally vegetarian.
  • Vegan: Increasingly accommodated, especially at higher-end restaurants and those in tourist areas. Traditional cuisine uses coconut milk rather than dairy in many dishes.
  • Gluten-Free: Understood at larger restaurants and resorts. Traditional cuisine is largely gluten-free, using taro, sweet potato, and rice instead of wheat.
  • Food Allergies: Major allergies are taken seriously at resorts and upscale restaurants. Always communicate your specific needs clearly.
  • Religious Dietary Restrictions: Halal options are limited but available at some restaurants. Kosher meals generally require special arrangements.

Tips for dining with dietary restrictions:

  • Research and contact restaurants in advance when possible
  • Learn relevant phrases in Tanitian or carry a dietary card in the local language
  • Inform your hotel of your needs; they can often make recommendations
  • Consider booking accommodations with kitchen facilities for greater control

Is the tap water safe to drink?

Tap water is generally safe to drink in the main town and at major resorts, which have modern water treatment facilities. However, in more remote areas of the island, water quality can vary.

For visitors, we recommend:

  • Drinking bottled water, which is widely available and inexpensive
  • Using the filtered water provided by most hotels and restaurants
  • Confirming with your accommodation about local water safety
  • Avoiding ice in remote areas unless you're certain it's made from purified water

If you're staying for an extended period or visiting remote areas, consider bringing a portable water filter or purification tablets to reduce plastic waste from bottled water.

Most restaurants and cafés serve complimentary filtered water with meals.

What is the tipping culture on Taniti?

Tipping is not traditionally part of Tanitian culture, but it has become more common in tourist areas. Here's a general guide:

  • Restaurants: A 10% service charge is often included at higher-end establishments. If not included, a 10-15% tip for good service is appreciated but not expected.
  • Hotels: $1-2 USD per bag for porters, $2-5 USD per day for housekeeping left in the room daily.
  • Tour Guides: 10-15% of the tour cost for a full-day tour with excellent service.
  • Taxis: Rounding up the fare or adding 10% is appreciated but not mandatory.
  • Spas: 15-20% for treatments if a service charge is not already included.

Always check your bill to see if a service charge has already been added. Tipping in local currency is preferred, but US dollars are widely accepted.

In more rural areas and at local establishments, tipping is less common and not expected, though always appreciated for exceptional service.

Local Customs

What are important cultural etiquette tips for visitors?

Respecting local customs will enhance your experience on Taniti. Here are important etiquette guidelines:

  • Greetings: A handshake is common, though some Tanitians may place their hand over their heart after shaking hands as a sign of respect. Learning simple greetings in the local language is appreciated.
  • Dress Code: Modest dress is appropriate when visiting villages, religious sites, and local communities. Cover shoulders and knees, and remove shoes when entering someone's home or a temple.
  • Photography: Always ask permission before photographing locals, especially during ceremonies or in villages. Some sacred sites prohibit photography.
  • Religious Respect: Remove hats and shoes when entering religious buildings. Speak quietly and avoid pointing at religious objects or images.
  • Body Language: Pointing with your finger is considered rude; use your whole hand or chin instead. Avoid touching someone's head, which is considered sacred.
  • Gifts: If invited to a local's home, bringing a small gift is appreciated. Fruit, sweets, or items from your home country are appropriate.
  • Environment: Respect for nature is deeply ingrained in Tanitian culture. Never litter, damage coral reefs, or disturb wildlife.

Tanitians are generally forgiving of cultural mistakes made by visitors, but showing an effort to respect local customs is always appreciated.

Are there any local laws or regulations I should be aware of?

While most laws are similar to those in Western countries, there are some specific regulations to be aware of:

  • Protected Areas: Many natural areas are protected. Stay on marked trails and follow all posted rules in nature reserves.
  • Marine Conservation: Coral collection, shell harvesting, and fishing in protected areas are strictly prohibited with heavy fines.
  • Wildlife Protection: Interacting with or feeding wildlife is generally prohibited outside of sanctioned activities.
  • Cultural Artifacts: Exporting antiques or artifacts over 100 years old is illegal without special permits.
  • Alcohol & Tobacco: The legal drinking age is 18. Smoking is prohibited in public buildings, restaurants, and on public transportation. Some beaches are designated non-smoking.
  • Drugs: All illegal drugs are strictly prohibited with severe penalties, including imprisonment.
  • Public Behavior: Public nudity is only permitted on designated beaches. Public intoxication can result in fines.
  • Photography Restrictions: Photography is prohibited at military installations, some government buildings, and certain sacred sites.

Penalties for breaking laws can include fines, deportation, or in serious cases, imprisonment. Respect for local laws and customs ensures a positive experience for both visitors and residents.

Are there any local festivals or events I should know about?

Taniti celebrates several vibrant festivals throughout the year that showcase the island's culture and traditions:

  • Harvest Festival (March): A week-long celebration of the island's agricultural bounty with food tastings, traditional cooking demonstrations, music, and dance performances.
  • Founders' Day (May 15): Commemorates the island's founding with historical reenactments, parades, and a spectacular evening fire dance ceremony in the main town.
  • Ocean Festival (July): Celebrates Taniti's connection to the sea with canoe races, swimming competitions, seafood feasts, and marine conservation activities.
  • Arts & Crafts Fair (September): Local artisans showcase traditional and contemporary crafts, with workshops where visitors can learn techniques.
  • New Year's Celebration (December 31-January 2): Three days of festivities combining traditional ceremonies with modern celebrations, culminating in beachfront fireworks.

Additionally, smaller village festivals occur throughout the year, often centered around fishing seasons, historical events, or religious observances. Your hotel concierge can provide information about any events happening during your stay.

If your visit coincides with a festival, we recommend booking accommodations well in advance, as these are popular times for both tourists and returning Tanitians.

What are appropriate souvenirs to bring home?

Taniti offers many authentic and ethically produced souvenirs that support local artisans and communities:

  • Handwoven Textiles: Colorful fabrics, scarves, and tapestries made using traditional techniques and natural dyes.
  • Wood Carvings: Intricate sculptures, masks, and decorative items crafted from sustainable local woods.
  • Jewelry: Pieces made from local materials like shells, coconut, seeds, and volcanic stone.
  • Tanitian Vanilla: Premium vanilla beans and extract, as Taniti is known for its high-quality vanilla production.
  • Coconut Products: Oils, soaps, and skincare items made from locally harvested coconuts.
  • Spices & Teas: Local blends of herbs, spices, and teas packaged for easy transport.
  • Artwork: Paintings and prints by local artists depicting island scenes and cultural themes.
  • Musical Instruments: Small drums, flutes, or percussion instruments used in traditional music.

The best places to shop for authentic souvenirs include:

  • The Artisan Market in the main town
  • Village craft cooperatives throughout the island
  • The Cultural Center gift shop
  • Certified Fair Trade shops that ensure artisans receive fair compensation

Avoid purchasing items made from endangered species, coral, or turtle shell, which are illegal to export and harmful to the environment.

What are some useful Tanitian phrases for visitors?

While English is widely spoken in tourist areas, learning a few phrases in the Tanitian language is appreciated by locals and can enhance your cultural experience:

  • Halo - Hello
  • Mahalo - Thank you
  • Mahalo nui - Thank you very much
  • Paliki - Please
  • Ae - Yes
  • Aole - No
  • Alofa - Welcome
  • Talofa - Goodbye
  • Kala mai - Excuse me/I'm sorry
  • Pehea 'oe? - How are you?
  • Maika'i - Good/I'm fine
  • E komo mai - Welcome (when inviting someone in)
  • Kai - Food
  • Wai - Water
  • Fale - House/building
  • Moana - Ocean

Pronunciation tips:

  • Vowels are pronounced similarly to Spanish: A (ah), E (eh), I (ee), O (oh), U (oo)
  • Every syllable is pronounced with equal emphasis
  • The glottal stop (') indicates a brief pause between vowels

Small pocket phrasebooks are available at the airport, visitor center, and bookstores around the island.
