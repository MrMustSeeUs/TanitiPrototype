TRANSPORTATION

Getting to and around Taniti is convenient with multiple transportation options available.

AIR

Most visitors arrive by air at our small airport that accommodates small jets and propeller planes. Airport expansion is underway to accommodate larger jets in the future.

A small cruise ship also docks in Yellow Leaf Bay one night per week.

BUS

Public buses serve Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily. Private buses provide service to the rest of the island.

TAXI

Taxis are readily available throughout Taniti City for convenient point-to-point transportation.

RENTALS

Rental cars are available from a local agency near the airport. Bikes and helmets (required by law) can be rented from several vendors around the island.

WALKING

Taniti City is flat and very walkable. The Merriton Landing area is also easy to explore on foot.

PRACTICAL INFORMATION

Essential information to help you prepare for your visit to Taniti Island.

CURRENCY

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.

More currency info

POWER

Power outlets on Taniti are 120 volts, the same as in the United States. Travelers from other regions may need adapters.

More power info

LANGUAGE

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.

More language info

HEALTH

There is one hospital and several clinics on the island. The hospital has many multilingual employees to assist visitors.

More health info

TRAVEL TIPS

  • Book accommodations well in advance during peak season (December-April)
  • Consider visiting during shoulder seasons (May-June, November) for fewer crowds
  • Pack light, breathable clothing and sun protection
  • Bring insect repellent for rainforest excursions
  • Check the holiday calendar when planning activities
  • Learn a few basic phrases in the local language
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when away from beaches
